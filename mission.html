The Mission

What is the issue?

Fertile soil has a pH range of roughly 5.5 - 6.5, with acidic soil being considered having a pH of less than 5.

This lowering of pH can be caused by several factors, but result in dire consequences for the stability of the soil. These include:

  • Decreased Nutrient Absorption, affecting:

  • Nitrogen

  • Potassium

  • Phosphorus

  • Increased likelihood of toxic metals dissolving in the soil (bad!)

Why limestone?

Limestone isn't a magical rock, it contains Calcium Carbonate. Calcium Carbonate, a chemical compound with formula CaCO3, interacts with acids to release Carbonic Acid (H2CO3), which disintergates into Carbon Dioxide and Water, as shown in the below equation:

CaCO3(s) + 2H+(aq) -> Ca2+(aq) + CO2(g) + H2O (l)

This in turn, as well as a result of the basicness of CaCO3, neutralizes acidic soil.
